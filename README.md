## Headline findings

- **No exchange ratio works.** At every commonly-discussed ratio (0.3x–0.7x), the deal is dilutive to Tesla shareholders on both EPS and EBITDA/share. The mathematical breakeven ratio is **0.13x** — far below anything SpaceX shareholders would accept.
- **It's a multiple-arbitrage problem, not a fairness problem.** SpaceX trades at ~394x EV/EBITDA vs. Tesla's ~103x. Issuing Tesla's "cheaper" stock for SpaceX's richer-multiple earnings is mechanically dilutive almost regardless of price.
- **Neither stock is explained by its own cash flows.** A 10-year DCF implies Tesla is worth $48.96/share (vs. $406.43 trading) and SpaceX $10.42/share (vs. $200.00 trading) — both priced almost entirely on AI/robotics optionality.
- **The governance math cuts against the deal happening at all.** A plain single-class merger would shrink Musk's voting control from ~83% (SpaceX today) to ~30–36% (combined entity) — a structural disincentive absent from most public commentary.
- **The market has roughly already priced this in.** A probability-weighted blend of deal/no-deal scenarios values SpaceX within ~1% of its current trading price.

**Bottom line:** Merger thesis NEUTRAL · Tesla HOLD ($410–450 PT) · SpaceX tactical TRIM ($164–190 base case PT).
